We. Love. Games.

Obscuratus Gaming is a collaborative gaming studio created by four friends who have always been obsessed with games.

We play, design, create, and sell games that bring people together.

Here at Obscuratus Gaming, we've played games of all kinds, all of our lives: individual and team sports, board games, video games, mobile games, and most recently, games involving virtual reality.

We've learned that games bring people together. They entertain and educate; they encourage imagination, curiosity, and a sense of adventure....and they delight.

Of course, they can also frustrate and annoy, and that’s why we’re here! We can help you find, and learn how to play, games of all kinds. We also develop new games.

We love hearing your gaming stories, your ideas for new games, and stories about games that are "old favorites." And, as we said above, we love playing games - and we look forward to hearing from you soon!


WHO WE ARE

We are four friends whose passion for games and gaming has led us far beyond what we ever imagined. We are constantly striving to develop more feature-rich, culture-immersive games to entertain, educate, and enthrall.

Cheyenne Alvarez

Cheyenne discovered computer and video games in elementary school and has recently begun a career in game development.

She loves designing game environments and characters, animation, and developing storylines.

Currently in the early stages of developing a game with an aquatic theme, Cheyenne will be leading our 2022 trip to Spain. Until then, she has been immersed in details related to African textiles and Russian music.

Favorite Game: Ark.

Amy Facca

Amy grew up playing sports and games with friends and family.

While relatively new to video, computer, and mobile games, she is adapting well and develops many of our game stories.

Amy is leading our 2020 trip to Japan, gathering new details about winter sports, the Japanese tea ceremony, calligraphy, and papermaking that will be used in another game currently in development.

Favorite Games: Trivial Pursuit, Tetris, Frogger, Donkey Kong, and Madden Football (thanks to her nephews!).

Jason Gish

A long-time gamer, Jason now enjoys introducing the world of gaming to his kids.

Among other things, he is developing engaging and entertaining games that teach kids about computer programming.

Jason led our trips to Russia and China, gathering details for educational games focused on culture and role playing games featuring the KGB and Chinese spy agencies.

Favorite Games: Tecmo Super Bowl and Zelda games.

Kyle Williams

Kyle grew up playing computer and video games.

Along the way, he began programming them too. He loves complexity, mystery, role play, and classic battles of good vs. evil.

From a military family, Kyle led our trip to Africa. Beyond our collective focus on heritage and culture, he gathered information and details for a role-playing game featuring CIA covert affairs related to the international scramble for territory and mineral rights.

Favorite Games: Skyrim and Minecraft.
